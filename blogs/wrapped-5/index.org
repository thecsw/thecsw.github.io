influence that shaped this year's /Wrapped/. Strap yourself in. #indiecore

Starting off with the basic stuff---I beat my last year's record of minutes
listened by almost 5,000 minutes. Woah. And thanks to my wife, my taste had
actually taken a walk outside and I have had the chance to finally branch out of
never-ending loop of anime soundtracks and Japanese alternative rock into more
``modern'' flavors of music.

When it comes to albums and say I've listened to 13 of them... do they mean like
front-to-back sort of deal or simply ``touched an album''? I'm sure it's the
former, because there is no way 1,800+ songs reduce down to measely 13 albums,
otherwise, those would have been some massive releases. Okay, this one is
simple. I go weak for Chapell Roan's /After Midnight/. That one progression and
note that she hits on the

#+begin_quote
/'Cause After Midniighttt/ \
/I'm feeling kinda freaky,/ \
/maybe it's the club lights/ ---
#+end_quote

I'm not the biggest club go-er. In actuality, I think the biggest number of them
that are easily available to go to are kinda trashy. I can explain. They are TOO
loud. The music is TOO generic. And there is no charm or grace to drinking in
these places. But the vibe that /After Midnight/ the song has, which I imagine as
a more desolate, emptier club, but where the vibes are just right---then yes,
please sign me up.
