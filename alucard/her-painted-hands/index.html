🍤 At least today ago

My dear reader, and one day, I hope, to call each other friends—welcome to the little corner I call my home. It’s not much, it’s not grandiose, it may not even be comfortable for some.

And I try, I do try, to make it home for all, but how can I if there is a universe sitting between a man and a man. Inasmuch you are still here, let me share with you one of my first collections of what I find fascinating.

Whether be the great works of art or the lesser known or the ones known at most to their authors—the human touch and gentleness they radiate intoxicates me.

A hand, a lip, the veins running down their arms, or be strand of hair seemingly out of place, but in the most attractive way; all the imperfections that make us perfect—that is what I want to discover.

Please join me on small adventures of what I want to share, selfishly, for myself, but if it brings joy to you, and that is more than I can ask, so I’m grateful.

One day, I hope to see you here again. My dear stranger.
