* Casino

As the casinos and mob-controlled enterprises had met their demise, so had the
``good ol' America,'' which compared to today's faceless enterprises that know
no bounds to their greed, had character, and some personality, which we can see
in full bloom in Martin Scorsese's /Casino/ topped to the brim with Joe Pesci's
violence---man, I would not want to be near that person ever, you know something
had gone wrong that some decisions had led to him---with De Niro's elegance, and
my, Sharon Stone's absolute femme fatale energy. /Casino/ occupies the same space
as /Wolf of Wall Street/, the death of entertainment business with a face on it.
