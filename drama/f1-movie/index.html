It would be hard to imagine a movie that is more formulaic and predictable than F1: The Movie, yet retains its Top Gun-style charm led by Brad Pitt’s affable performance and the story so inoffensive and straightforward that the total experience, albeit forgettable, is a positive one. The entire setup of our hero being a nomad guy who does it not for the fame and glory (anymore), but for the sake of driving of in itself. Writing this after watching it a month or so ago, I can’t recall that much in terms of details or anything that stood out to me—perhaps, it left the same aftertaste in my mouth as Cars 3, with the young beating the old with the new tech and gadgets, but the old besting them in lived experience.
